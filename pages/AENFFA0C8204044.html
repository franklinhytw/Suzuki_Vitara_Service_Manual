DTC Troubleshooting
Step Action Yes No
1
Was “Air Bag Diagnostic System Check” performed?
Go to Step 2.
Go to Air Bag Diagnostic System Check.
2
Knee air bag (inflator) module check
1)Check that ignition is “OFF”.
2)Check for proper connection to “G222” connector.
3)Disconnect “G222” connector.  
4)Connect “STEERING WHEEL” (1) of special tool (C), special tools (A) and (B) to “G222” connector.
Special Tool
(A): 09932–76010
(B): 09932–78310
(C): 09932–75011
5)Set ignition “ON”.
6)Check for DTC.  
Is DTC B106E-1B indicated?
Go to Step 3.
Replace knee air bag (inflator) module.  
3
Knee air bag (inflator) module circuit check
1)Set ignition “OFF”.
2)Check for proper connection to “L05” connector.
3)Disconnect “L05” connector.  
4)Release shorting bar in SDM connector inserting release tool (B).
5)Check the following points.
•Resistance between “L05-21” and “G222-1” terminals: 1 Ω or less
•Resistance between “L05-22” and “G222-2” terminals: 1 Ω or less
Special Tool
(A): 09932–76010
(B): 09932–75100
Is check result OK?
Replace SDM and recheck DTC.  
Go to Step 4.
4
Instrument panel harness circuit check
1)Check for proper connection to “L375” connector.
2)Disconnect “L375” connector.
3)Check the following point.
•Resistance of “A1” and “A2” circuits: 1 Ω or less
Is check result OK?
Replace floor harness.
Replace instrument panel harness.
NOTE:
Upon completion of inspection and repair work, perform the following items.
•Reconnect all air bag system components and check that all components are properly mounted.
•Clear DTCs, referring to DTC Clearance if any DTC is detected.
•Repeat Air Bag Diagnostic System Check to check that the trouble has been solved.
