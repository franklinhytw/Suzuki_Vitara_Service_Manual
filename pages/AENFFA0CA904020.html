DTC Detecting Condition and Trouble Area

P1311: ENG A-STOP Starter Relay Circuit Low
Measured voltage of ST2 relay drive circuit is not as specified for specified time continuously.
•“ST” fuse
•ST2 relay circuit / connector
•ST2 relay
•ENG A-STOP control module
P1312: ENG A-STOP Starter Relay Circuit High
Measured voltage of ST2 relay drive circuit is not as specified for specified time continuously.
Circuit Diagram
[A]: ENG A-STOP control module connector (View: [a]) 1. ENG A-STOP control module 4. “ST” fuse
A1: ST2 relay drive circuit 2. ST2 relay    
A2: ST2 relay signal circuit 3. Starting motor    
DTC Confirmation Procedure
1)Set ignition “ON”.
DTC Troubleshooting
Step Action Yes No
1
ST2 relay drive circuit and signal circuit check
1)Check that ignition is “OFF”.
2)Remove ST2 relay.
3)Disconnect “E24” connector.
4)Check for proper terminal connection to “E24” and ST2 relay connectors.
5)If connections are OK, check the following points.
•Resistance of “A1” and “A2” circuits: less than 1 Ω
•Resistance between each of “A1” and “A2” circuit and ground: infinity
•Resistance between each of “A1” and “A2” circuit terminals and other terminal at ST2 relay connector: infinity
•Voltage of “A1” and “A2” circuits: approx. 0 V (When ignition is “ON”)
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 2.
Repair or replace defective wire harness.
2
ST2 relay check
1)Check ST2 relay.  
Is check result OK?
Replace ENG A-STOP control module and recheck for DTC.  
Replace ST2 relay.
