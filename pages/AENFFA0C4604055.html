ESP® Control Module Power Supply and Ground Circuit Check
Circuit Diagram
[A]: ESP® control module connector (View: [a]) 1. Lead to battery 7. Lead to solenoid valve power supply relay
A1: Solenoid valve power supply circuit 2. Relay box 8. “ABS” fuse (25A)
A2: Pump motor power supply circuit 3. J/B 9. “ABS2” fuse
B1: Ignition power supply circuit 4. ESP® control module 10. “ABS” fuse (10A)
C1: ESP® control module ground circuit 1 5. Steering angle sensor 11. Ignition power supply
C2: ESP® control module ground circuit 2 6. Lead to pump motor relay    
Circuit Description
When the ignition is set “ON”, power is supplied to ESP® control module. Then power is supplied to pump motor and solenoid valve in other line by the switching function in ESP® control module.
Troubleshooting
Step Action Yes No
1
Fuse check
Are “ABS” fuse (25A), “ABS2” fuse and “ABS” fuse (10A) in good condition?
Go to Step 2.
Replace fuse and check for short circuit to ground.
2
ESP® control module power supply circuit check
1)Check that ignition is “OFF”.
2)Disconnect “E08” connector.
3)Check for proper connection to “E08” connector.
4)If connections are OK, set ignition “ON”.
5)Measure voltage between “E08-4” terminal and ground.
Is it 10 – 14 V?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace “B1” circuit.
3
Pump motor and solenoid valve power supply circuit check
1)Check that ignition is “OFF”.
2)Measure voltage between each of “E08-26” and “E08-1” terminals and ground.
Are they 10 – 14 V?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace “A1” circuit and/or “A2” circuit.
4
ESP® control module ground circuit check
1)Measure resistance between each of “E08-14” and “E08-38” terminals and ground.
Is resistance less than 1 Ω?
ESP® control module power supply and ground circuits are in good condition.
Repair or replace “C1” circuit and/or “C2” circuit.
