Generator Symptom Diagnosis
Trouble in charging system will show up as one or more of the following conditions.
Charging Warning Light Operation
NOTE:
When ENG A-STOP system stops engine in normal condition, charge warning light does not light up.
Check “Charge Light” in Scan Tool Data to identify easily most possible cause of trouble. “Charge Light” indicates command signal of charge warning light from ECM to combination meter.
Condition Possible Cause Action
Charge warning light does not light with ignition “ON” and engine off
Blown fuse
Replace fuse.
Faulty combination meter
Replace combination meter.  
Faulty ECM
Check ECM.  
Faulty CAN communication line
Repair or replace CAN communication line.  
Faulty wiring
Repair or replace wiring.
Faulty IC regulator or field coil
Repair or replace generator.  
Charge warning light does not go out with engine running (battery requires frequent recharging)
Loose or worn drive belt
Adjust or replace drive belt.  
Faulty combination meter
Replace combination meter.  
Faulty ECM
Check ECM.  
Faulty IC regulator or generator
Repair or replace generator.  
Faulty wiring
Repair or replace wiring.

Battery Charge Failure
Condition Possible Cause Action
Undercharged battery
Loose or worn drive belt
Adjust or replace drive belt.  
Loose or corroded battery terminal
Tighten battery terminal or replace battery.  
Faulty rectifier, rotor and/or IC regulator
Repair or replace generator.  
Overcharged battery
Faulty IC regulator
Repair or replace generator.  

Noise
Condition Possible Cause Action
Noisy generator
Loose mounting bolts
Tighten mounting bolts.  
Dirty, worn and/or faulty bearings
Repair or replace generator.  
Faulty rotor, diode and/or stator
Repair or replace generator.
