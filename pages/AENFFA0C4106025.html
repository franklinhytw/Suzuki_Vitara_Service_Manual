Brake Booster Pressure Sensor Inspection (ENG A-STOP Model)
NOTICE:
If excessive vacuum is applied, the brake booster pressure sensor may be damaged.
Do not exceed a vacuum of –100 kPa (–1.0 kgf/cm2, –14.5 psi, –1.0 bar).
1)Remove brake booster pressure sensor.  
2)Check brake booster pressure sensor output voltage as follows.
If check result is not as specified, replace brake booster pressure sensor.
a)Arrange 3 new 1.5 V batteries (2) in series and check that total voltage is 4.5 – 5.0 V.
b)Connect special tool, voltmeter (3) and batteries to brake booster pressure sensor (1) as shown in figure.
Special Tool
(A): 09917–47011
4. Adapter included in special tool
c)Check that voltage varies gradually when negative pressure is gradually applied with special tool.
Vacuum sensor reference voltage
Negative pressure 0 kPa (0.0 kgf/cm2, 0.0 psi, 0.0 bar): Approx. 0.5 V
Negative pressure –100 kPa (–1.0 kgf/cm2, –14.5 psi, –1.0 bar): Approx. 3.0 V
