Troubleshooting
NOTE:
•For details of engine cooling fan operation, refer to Cooling Fan Operation Description:M16A.
•Before beginning troubleshooting, read Precautions for Engine (M16A).
•When measuring circuit voltage, resistance and/or pulse signal of ECM, refer to Inspection of ECM and Its Circuits:M16A.

1)Check radiator cooling fan control for high-speed operation.  
Is check result OK?

4
Radiator cooling fan relay No.1 power supply circuit (switch side) check
1)Set ignition “OFF” and remove radiator cooling fan relay No.1 from individual circuit fuse box No.1.
2)Check for proper connection to radiator cooling fan relay No.1 at each terminal.
3)If OK, measure voltage between “A1” circuit and ground.
Is it 10 – 14 V?
Go to Step 5.
Repair “A1” circuit.
5
Radiator cooling fan relay No.1 check
1) Check radiator cooling fan relay No.1.  
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 6.
Replace radiator cooling fan relay No.1.
6
Radiator cooling fan motor drive circuit (low speed) check
1)Set ignition “OFF” and disconnect radiator cooling fan motor connector.
2)Check for proper connection to radiator cooling fan motor at each terminal.
3)If OK, check the following points.
•Resistance of “B1” circuit: 1 Ω or less
•Resistance between “B1” circuit and ground: infinity
•Voltage of “B1” circuit: approx. 0 V (With ignition is “ON”)
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 7.
Repair “B1” circuit.
7
Radiator cooling fan motor ground circuit check
1)Measure resistance between “C1” circuit and ground.
Is resistance 1 Ω or less?
Go to Step 14.
Repair “C1” circuit.
8

3)If OK, measure voltage between “A2” circuit and ground.
Is it 10 – 14 V?
