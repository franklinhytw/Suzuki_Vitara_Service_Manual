Fuel Tank Removal and Installation
Reference: Fuel System Components:D16AA
Reference: Fuel Tank On-Vehicle Inspection:D16AA
Before performing the following procedure, observe Precautions for Fuel System Service:D16AA.
Removal
1)Relieve fuel pressure.  
2)Disconnect negative (–) cable at battery.
3)Remove fuel filler cap.
4)Insert hose of a hand operated pump into fuel filler hose (1) and drain fuel in space “a” as shown in figure.
WARNING:
There is a risk of fire or explosion due to static electricity if the drained fuel is stored in an open container or plastic container.
Store the fuel in a sealed metal container.
NOTICE:
The hose of the hand operated pump could damage the fuel tank inlet valve if excessive force is applied when inserting or removing it from the fuel tank.
Do not apply excessive force when inserting or removing the hose of the hand operated pump.
5)Hoist vehicle and remove clamp (1).
6)Remove breather hose (2) from fuel filler neck and turn plastic clamp (3).
NOTICE:
The plastic clamp could be damaged if excessive force is applied when turning it. Turn plastic clamp carefully.
7)Remove clamp (1) and fuel filler hose (2) from fuel filler neck.
8)Remove exhaust center pipe.  
9)For 4WD model, remove propeller shaft.  
10)Disconnect fuel pipes (1) from fuel tank (2).
11)Support fuel tank (1) with jack (2) and remove fuel tank bolts.
12)Lower fuel tank (1) to a height at which tension is not applied to the fuel pump harness (4) and keep quick joint (fuel pipe) (3) level with top of the fuel filler neck (6) to prevent fuel from flowing out after disconnecting quick joint (fuel pipe).
NOTICE:
Applying tension to the fuel pump harness (4) will cause damage to the fuel pump (5) and the fuel pump harness when lowering the fuel tank.
Disconnect the fuel feed hose, the fuel return hose and/or the fuel pump connector with the fuel tank lowered to a height at which tension is not applied to the fuel pump harness.
13)Disconnect fuel return hose (1) and fuel feed hose (2) and then install special tool to prevent fuel from flowing out after disconnecting quick joints.
Special Tool
(A): 09919–46310
14)Disconnect fuel pump connector (1).
15)Disconnect fuel pipes (2).  
16)Remove fuel tank from vehicle.
Installation
Reference: Fuel Tank Inspection:D16AA
Reference: Fuel Tank Flushing Procedure:D16AA
WARNING:
Failure to take proper precautions when reinstalling the fuel tank connections can result in fuel leakage or damage.
Clean the outside surface of the pipe where each quick joint is to be installed, push the pipe into the joint until the joint lock clicks, and then check that the pipe is connected securely.
1)If parts have been removed from fuel tank, install them before installing fuel tank to vehicle.
2)Connect fuel feed pipe (2) and fuel return pipe (1) to fuel pump.  
3)Raise fuel tank (1) with jack, connect fuel pump / fuel level sensor connector and clamp wire harness.
4)Install fuel tank to vehicle, and tighten new fuel tank bolts (2) to specified torque.
NOTICE:
The fuel tank bolts are pre-coated with friction stabilizer. If the bolts are reused, they may work loose.
Use the new fuel tank bolts.
Tightening torque
Fuel tank bolt (a): 45 N·m (4.6 kgf-m, 33.5 lbf-ft)
5)Connect fuel filler hose (2) to fuel filler neck and clamp (1) them securely.
For proper installation, refer to Fuel Hose Disconnection and Reconnection:D16AA.
6)Turn plastic clamp (1).
NOTICE:
The plastic clamp could be damaged if excessive force is applied when turning it.
Turn plastic clamp carefully.
7)Connect breather hose (2) to fuel filler neck and clamp them securely.
For proper installation, refer to Fuel Hose Disconnection and Reconnection:D16AA.
8)Connect fuel return hose (1) and fuel feed hose (2) to each pipe as shown in figure and clamp them securely.  
9)Install exhaust center pipe.  
10)For 4WD model, install propeller shaft.  
11)Connect negative (–) cable at battery.
12)Check for fuel leakage.
