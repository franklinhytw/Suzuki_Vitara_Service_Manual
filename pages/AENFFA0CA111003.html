10A
Description of Cruise Control System Cancel Conditions
Constant cruising is canceled under one of the following conditions.
•CRUISE (main) switch is turned OFF.
•*Vehicle speed becomes lower than minimum operating speed (36 km/h (22 mile/h)).
•*Actual vehicle speed is continuously lower than set vehicle speed by a specified amount.
•*Brake pedal is depressed, that is; brake light switch is turned ON.
•*Clutch pedal is depressed, that is; clutch pedal position switch is turned ON. (M/T model)
•*Gear shift lever is in 1st or 2nd position. (M/T model)
•*Select lever is shifted to other than “D” or “M” range. (A/T model / twin clutch system model)
•*Select lever is in “M” range and gear position is in 1st or 2nd position. (A/T model / twin clutch system model)
•*CANCEL switch is turned ON.
•*ESP® is operating.
•Acceleration or deceleration is out of the specified value.
•Speed limiter system is turned ON. (if equipped)
NOTE:
When constant cruising is canceled due to a condition with * (asterisk), vehicle speed before cancellation can be resumed by operating RES/+ switch, provided that vehicle speed is higher than 40 km/h (25 mile/h).
