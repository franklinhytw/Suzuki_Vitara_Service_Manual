DTC Detecting Condition and Trouble Area
DTC detecting condition Trouble area
P0036: HO2S Heater Control Circuit (Sensor 2)
HO2S-2 heater drive circuit is open.
(3 D/C detection logic)
•HO2S-2 heater and/or its circuit
•ECM
P0037: HO2S Heater Control Circuit Low (Sensor 2)
HO2S-2 heater drive circuit is shorted to ground.
(3 D/C detection logic)
P0038: HO2S Heater Control Circuit High (Sensor 2)
HO2S-2 heater drive circuit is shorted to power supply.
(3 D/C detection logic)

DTC Troubleshooting
Step Action Yes No
1
Was “Engine and Emission Control System Check” performed?

2
HO2S-2 heater power supply circuit check
1)With ignition “OFF”, disconnect HO2S-2 connector.
2)Check for proper terminal connection to HO2S-2 connector.
3)If connection is OK, set ignition “ON”.
4)Check that voltage between “B1” circuit and ground is battery voltage.
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace defective wiring harness.
3
HO2S-2 heater drive circuit check
1)With ignition “OFF”, disconnect “C01” and “E01” connectors from ECM.
2)Check for proper terminal connection to “C01” and “E01” connectors.
3)If connections are OK, check the following points.
•Resistance of “B4” circuit: less than 1 Ω
•Resistance between “B4” circuit and ground: infinity
•Resistance between “B4” circuit terminal and other terminal at HO2S-2 connector: infinity
•Voltage of “B4” circuit: approx. 0 V (When ignition is “ON”)
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace defective wiring harness.
4
HO2S-2 check
1)Check HO2S-2 heater.  
Is check result OK?
Replace ECM and recheck DTC.
