2
ECM power supply circuit and ground circuit check
1)When ignition is “OFF”, disconnect “E01” connector from ECM and “FI” fuse (30 A).
2)Check for proper terminal connection to “E01” connector.
3)If connections are OK, check that ECM power supply circuit and ground circuit (“B1”, “B2”, “B3”, “C1”, “C2” and “C3”) is as follows.
•Resistance of “B1”, “B2”, “B3”, “C1”, “C2” and “C3” circuits: less than 1 Ω
•Resistance between each of “B1”, “B2”, “B3”, “C1”, “C2” and “C3” circuits and ground: infinity
•Resistance between “B1” circuit terminal and other terminal at ECM connector: infinity
•Resistance between “B2” circuit terminal and other terminal at ECM connector: infinity
•Resistance between “B3” circuit terminal and other terminal at ECM connector: infinity
•Resistance between “C1” circuit terminal and other terminal at ECM connector: infinity
•Resistance between “C2” circuit terminal and other terminal at ECM connector: infinity
•Resistance between “C3” circuit terminal and other terminal at ECM connector: infinity
•Voltage of “B1”, “B2”, “B3”, “C1”, “C2” and “C3” circuits: approx. 0 V (When ignition is “ON”)
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace defective wire harness.
