DTC Detecting Condition and Trouble Area
DTC detecting condition Trouble area
C1250: Coupling Air Temperature Sensor Open
Input signal voltage from rear drive coupling air temperature sensor is higher than specified value for specified time.
•Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor
•Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor circuit
•4WD control module
C1251: Coupling Air Temperature Sensor Short
Input signal voltage from rear drive coupling air temperature sensor is lower than specified value for specified time.
Circuit Diagram
[A]: 4WD control module connector (View: [a]) 1. Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor
A1: Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor signal circuit 2. 4WD control module
A2: Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor ground circuit    
DTC Confirmation Procedure
1)Set ignition “ON”.
DTC Troubleshooting
Step Action Yes No
1
Was “4WD control system check” performed?
Go to Step 2.
Go to 4WD Control System Check.
2
Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor signal circuit and rear drive coupling air temperature sensor ground circuit check
1)Disconnect “L14” and rear drive coupling air temperature sensor connectors with ignition “OFF”.
2)Check for proper connection to 4WD control module and rear drive coupling air temperature sensor.
3)If connections are OK, check the following points.
•Resistance of “A1” and “A2” circuits: less than 1 Ω
•Resistance between “A1” circuit and ground: infinity
•Resistance between “A1” circuit terminal and other terminal at rear drive coupling air temperature sensor connector: infinity
•Voltage of “A1” circuit: approx. 0 V (When ignition is “ON”)
Is check result OK?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace defective wire harness.
3
Rear drive coupling air temperature sensor check
1)Check rear drive coupling air temperature sensor.  
Is check result OK?
Substitute a known-good 4WD control module and recheck DTC.  
Replace rear drive coupling air temperature sensor.
